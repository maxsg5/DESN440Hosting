Project Overview

Project Description

Atlantis Short Film Festival immerses its audience through the power of storytelling in various genres. Emerging filmmakers' works are dynamically showcased on the website– hoping to inspire future filmmakers. Atlantis Short Film Festival aims to bolster and nurture amateur filmmakers, while we create a dynamic and captivating hub to showcase various films.

Client Project Statement

Goals Audience Important Features

We are excited to launch the [Atlantis] Short Film Festival's first website, a dynamic digital platform aimed at celebrating and showcasing the talents of amateur filmmakers in our region. We've had enormous success running our festival offline for a very supportive and enthusiastic amateur filmmaking audience for the past three years, and now our vision is to create an engaging, user-friendly website using WebFlow CMS, ensuring ease of content management for our team with minimal technical expertise.

The site will feature web-specific branding and a basic logo mark, reflecting the festival's creative spirit. A key aspect of the website is a streamlined digital submission process, opening annually from September 1 to October 15, to attract a diverse range of film genres and encourage participation from approximately 10-30 creators, resulting in an estimated 20-50 submissions. The website will also prominently display the previous 3 years’ winning films in each category to inspire and attract new submissions, and showcase them to an online audience. We aim to categorize these submissions into 3-7 genres, with the top three or so in each category being highlighted. We ask that your team chooses the categories – we will commit to whatever genres fit your own preference and which you think will resonate best with the audience.

Our goal is to launch the website by this summer, creating a buzz for the festival and making the entire film submission and viewing process free and accessible (we are generously sponsored by PBS.org and would like to acknowledge that on our website), with winners announced at the festival (and published on the website) on December 1. This project is not just about building a website; it's about crafting a digital home that celebrates the art of filmmaking and nurtures a community of emerging talents. 🎬
